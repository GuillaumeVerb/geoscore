# GeoScore — blog strategy (disciplined, product-led)

**Context:** GeoScore is deployed; scoring stays frozen (`AGENTS.md`). This document frames a **small** blog — not a media company, not an “AI SEO hacks” channel. It aligns with `vision.md`, `positioning.md`, `product-strengthening-plan.md`, and the bounded V2 story in `roadmap-v2-v3.md` (projects, compare, single-page depth — not unbounded crawl).

---

## A. Blog purpose

### Why the blog exists

- **Credibility:** Show that GeoScore’s worldview (SEO + GEO, explainability, limitations) is thought through — not a thin wrapper on a black box.
- **Acquisition:** Earn searches from people who already have the *problem* (unclear pages, weak extractability, pre-launch checks) and need language to act — then offer a concrete next step (analyze a URL).
- **GEO positioning:** Own sober, precise language around “readiness to be understood and cited” without buzzword bingo.
- **Education:** Reduce confusion between classic SEO readiness and what “GEO” means *in practice* for a single page snapshot.

### What it should do for the product

- Pull the **right** readers (founders, operators, small agencies — see positioning) toward **trying** GeoScore when a post naturally ends in “measure it on your URL.”
- Reinforce **explainable scoring**, **confidence**, **limitations**, and **page-type awareness** — the same story as `/how-it-works` and the live result UI.
- Support **short V2** narratives where they are true today: **projects**, **rescan**, **compare to previous run** — without promising roadmap items that are not shipped (e.g. no multi-page bundle posts until that exists).

### What it should NOT become

- A **keyword factory** or generic SEO content mill.
- “**AI visibility**” hype, secret ranking formulas, or guaranteed citations.
- A **news** or **trend** blog disconnected from the product.
- A substitute for **in-product** honesty (limitations, confidence) or for **docs** that belong next to the engine.
- A channel that implies GeoScore is a **rank tracker**, **backlink tool**, or **enterprise crawler** — it is not (`positioning.md`).

## B. Audience

### Who the blog is for

- **Primary:** SaaS founders, indie hackers, growth operators, solo consultants, small agencies (`positioning.md`).
- **Secondary:** Startup marketing, content teams, product marketers — when the topic is *page readiness*, not “SEO industry drama.”

### What problems they have

- “Our page *looks* fine but conversions / clarity / search snippets feel weak.”
- “We’re launching pricing / docs / a homepage rewrite — what should we sanity-check?”
- “People keep saying ‘GEO’ — what does that mean for *my* page, without snake oil?”
- “We changed the hero — did we improve *anything* measurable?” (before/after thinking — ties to rescan + compare where product supports it.)
- “We need a **shareable** diagnostic for a cofounder or client” — ties to public reports.

### What they are likely searching for (examples of intent, not a keyword list)

- SEO vs GEO difference (serious, non-fluffy).
- Homepage / pricing page / documentation page **readiness** or **checklist** (practical, bounded).
- Citation readiness, extractability, clarity (problem language — not “hack ChatGPT”).
- Before/after page changes, **without** promising rank outcomes.
- “Explainable” or “transparent” scoring vs black-box tools (positioning angle).

## C. Content pillars (max 5)

**Pillar 1 — SEO vs GEO, clearly and honestly**  
Teach the split GeoScore uses: classic search readiness vs understandability / citation-style signals from the **same observable snapshot**. No collapse into one vague “AI score.”

**Pillar 2 — Page-type lens (single URL)**  
Homepage vs pricing vs docs vs long article: what “good” tends to mean for **structure, clarity, and trust signals** — always bounded to what a single-page analyzer can credibly discuss (aligned with page type in the product).

**Pillar 3 — Explainability: confidence, limitations, fixes**  
Why deterministic-first matters; why partial runs and limitations are a feature, not an embarrassment; how to read issues and **priority** fixes — mirrors `how-it-works` and result UI.

**Pillar 4 — Iteration: rescan, compare, projects (product-true only)**  
Before/after as a **workflow story**: same URL over time, grouping by project, opening compare after rescan — **presentation diff**, not a second secret engine (`roadmap-v2-v3.md`). Do not write multi-page bundle tutorials until that ships.

**Pillar 5 — Practical “mini-audit” framing (anti-bloat)**  
How to do a **fast**, credible pass on one important URL without buying a bloated suite — philosophy aligned with GeoScore, not a 200-point enterprise checklist.

## D. Content rules

### Tone

- **Precise, sober, credible, modern** — same as `positioning.md` tone guidelines.
- Warm but not salesy; confident but not arrogant; no pseudo-science.

### Level of detail

- **One sharp idea per post** where possible; prefer 800–1,500 words over sprawling guides unless the topic truly needs depth.
- When technical, tie claims to **observable page properties** (headings, meta, structure, clarity) — not to proprietary “AI ranking secrets.”

### How product-led it should be

- **Every post** should have a clear line to “here is how you’d **see** this on a real URL” — usually GeoScore, occasionally a manual check *then* GeoScore.
- **At least one** intentional link to product surfaces (landing `#analyze`, `/how-it-works`, `/report/demo-example`, or `/pricing` when relevant) in the body or a short end box — not a banner farm.

### Jargon

- Define terms on first use (GEO, extractability, citation readiness).
- Avoid vendor-specific myth-making; avoid acronym soup without payoff.

### Claims to avoid

- Guaranteed rankings, AI citations, or “we know how model X ranks you.”
- Implying GeoScore **crawls the whole site** or replaces human judgment on brand/legal copy.
- Promising features **not in the product** (see roadmap before writing).

### Connecting to GeoScore without sounding forced

- **End with a concrete action:** “Paste your URL in GeoScore” / “Open the example report to see the same structure on sample data.”
- **Use the product as illustration:** “A GeoScore result surfaces X as issues Y — here is what that means on your pricing page.”
- **Acknowledge limits:** if the post discusses something only partially observable in a snapshot, say so — matches the product’s limitations story.

## G. Suggested blog information architecture

### Categories

- **Optional but useful:** 3–4 categories mirroring pillars (e.g. `seo-vs-geo`, `page-types`, `explainability`, `iteration`) — **not** 20 taxonomies. If simpler: **no categories** at first; only a flat `/blog` index + slug. Add categories only when ~8+ posts exist.

### Slug conventions

- Short, readable, hyphenated: `/blog/seo-vs-geo-one-page`, `/blog/homepage-readiness-bounded-checklist`.
- Avoid date-first slugs (avoid `/blog/2026/04/...` as primary) unless you strongly want archive semantics — prefer **topic-first** for evergreen acquisition.

### Author and date

- **Date:** yes (builds trust, signals freshness for evolving product) — `Updated` optional on major rewrites.
- **Author:** simple byline (name or “GeoScore team”) — no fake personas; **one** consistent voice aligns with sober brand.

### CTA blocks

- **One** soft CTA per post (text link + optional single button style) — e.g. “Analyze your URL” → `/#analyze`. Avoid mid-article interrupt banners.

### Screenshots / examples

- Use **sparingly**: redacted or synthetic when needed; prefer **example report** link over fragile screenshots that go stale. If showing UI, crop tightly and caption “illustrative.”

## H. Blog-to-product linkage (not salesy)

1. **Open with the problem** the reader already feels (clarity, launch risk, client conversation).
2. **Teach the frame** (SEO vs GEO, page type, limitations) — same language as `/how-it-works`.
3. **Land on a check** they can do in 2 minutes: GeoScore on their URL, or example report if they are not signed in.
4. **Close with one sentence** on what they will see (scores, issues, confidence) — no pressure, no countdown timers.
5. **Pricing** only when the post touches limits, teams, or “why not free forever” — link to `/pricing` as **direction**, not a hard sell.

## I. Out of scope (for now)

- Industry **news**, drama, or algorithm chase content.
- Broad **“media strategy”** or daily publishing cadence.
- **Unrelated AI** topics (prompt libraries, generic LLM productivity) with no line to page readiness.
- **Competitor teardowns** as a serial format (high noise, low product signal).
- Posts that **promise** roadmap features (multi-page bundle, competitor compare, scheduling) before they exist in the app.
- **Keyword-first** calendars disconnected from product truth.

## J. Smallest implementation approach (optional, in-app)

Aligned with **Next.js on Vercel** (`README.md`):

| Choice | Recommendation |
|--------|------------------|
| **Architecture** | **MDX or Markdown** in-repo under e.g. `frontend/content/blog/*.mdx` (or `content/blog/`) + one dynamic route `app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx` and `app/blog/page.tsx` index. No headless CMS required for v1. |
| **Where posts live** | Git-tracked files — review in PR, same cadence as product. |
| **MD enough?** | **Yes** for v1: Markdown + frontmatter (`title`, `description`, `date`, `updated?`, `pillar?`) is enough; MDX if you need occasional React components (e.g. CTA). |
| **Keep simple** | No comments, no tags explosion, no related-posts algorithm — **manual** “read next” links in frontmatter or footer. |
| **Nav** | Single **Blog** link in global nav to `/blog` — **live** with first post at `/blog/seo-vs-geo-one-page` (static route + index; expand to MD/MDX when post count grows). |
| **SEO** | Use Next metadata from frontmatter; one OG image template optional later. |

This keeps the blog **tight, product-led, and credible** — a disciplined extension of GeoScore, not a second product.
